Technical Approach

Enhanced Oil Recovery Strategy

  • Gas Re-injection: Produced solution gas re-injected into reservoir to improve oil displacement and recovery
  • NGL Extraction: Butane stripped from gas stream (~6 bbls per MMcf) for sale
  • Gas Storage: Reservoir acts as natural gas storage; gas produced after oil depletion
  • Modern Drilling: Horizontal wells using environmentally-safe drilling fluids

Why Enhanced Recovery is Necessary

Historical Challenges

  • Long production history (since 1871)
  • Significant reservoir depletion
  • Outdated drilling techniques used historically
  • Air drilling, no casing, open-hole completions
  • Limited modern production data

Modern Solutions

  • Horizontal drilling technology
  • Gas gun and foam stimulation
  • Cased and cemented wells
  • Environmentally-safe fluids
  • Pad drilling efficiency (8 wells/pad)

Recommendation

Investment Thesis: The property represents a high-risk, high-reward opportunity with significant upside potential if the Enhanced Oil Recovery technology proves successful. The phased development approach allows for intelligent capital allocation and risk management.

Recommended Path Forward

STEP 1: Proof of Concept ($120K)

  • Complete two existing wells
  • Validate production rates and reservoir characteristics
  • Collect modern production data
  • Timeline: 2-3 months
  • Decision Point: Proceed to Phase II if production meets expectations

STEP 2: EOR Pilot Program ($500K)

  • Install compressor and gas injection system
  • Test gas re-injection EOR on two wells
  • Monitor production response for 9-12 months
  • Timeline: 12-15 months total
  • Decision Point: Full development contingent on 3x+ production increase

STEP 3: Full Development ($15.15M)

  • Proceed ONLY if Phases I & II validate assumptions
  • Secure project financing based on proven results
  • Drill 5 pods over 12-18 month period
  • Expected IRR: 15-25% (if successful)
  • Payback Period: 5-7 years

Critical Success Factors

  • Phase I wells produce at projected rates (10+ bbl/d)
  • EOR increases production 3x+ (to 30 bbl/d)
  • Reservoir pressure maintains for gas injection
  • Oil prices remain above $55/bbl (break-even ~$45/bbl)
  • Capital costs remain within 10% of estimates
